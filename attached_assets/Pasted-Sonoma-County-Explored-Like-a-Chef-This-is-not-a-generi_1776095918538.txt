Sonoma County, Explored Like a Chef.

This is not a generic travel app. There is no algorithm deciding what gets recommended. No sponsored listings. No paid placements. Every winery, restaurant, farm, and farmstand on this map was chosen by hand — by a working chef who has eaten at these tables, visited these farms, and tasted what these winemakers are actually doing.

THE MAP

Open the app and you're looking at Sonoma County the way a chef sees it. Interactive pins mark every curated spot — color-coded by category, organized by region. Zoom into the Dry Creek Valley and find the small-production wineries that sell out before anyone hears about them. Drop into Sebastopol and see which farmstand is picking Gravensteins right now. Pull up Geyserville and know exactly which table to book for dinner.

Every pin tells you what the place actually is, what to order, what makes it worth the drive. Real notes. Honest opinions. The kind of thing a chef friend texts you before you visit.

WINERIES

From the Alexander Valley's old-vine Zinfandels to the cool-climate Pinot Noirs of the Sonoma Coast, the wine coverage here goes deep. Boutique producers alongside the estates that built this region's reputation. Tasting room hours, reservation requirements, what to ask for when you get there.

RESTAURANTS

Two Michelin-starred rooms and neighborhood joints where the pasta costs twelve dollars and tastes like someone made it this morning — because they did. The coverage spans the whole county, from Enclos in Sonoma (two Michelin stars, a Green Star, and the hottest reservation in Northern California) to Diavola in Geyserville (the wood-fired pizza and house charcuterie you'll think about for weeks). Every price point. Every corner of the county.

FARMS & FARMSTANDS

The farms are where this app goes somewhere no travel guide bothers to go. The Walker family's 70-year-old Sebastopol apple operation. A monastic community in Annapolis that cold-presses olive oil and runs an honor-system stand at 8 a.m. Lazaro Calderon's 6-acre plot inside Sonoma city limits, where the heirloom tomatoes in August are worth rearranging your schedule for. These are the places that supply the restaurants on this map — and now you can go direct.

THE SONOMA CHEF

Have a question? Ask. The built-in AI assistant knows this county cold. Ask what's in season right now. Ask for a wine to pair with the duck you're making tonight. Ask where to eat on a Monday when half the county is closed. Ask for a half-day itinerary through West County. The Sonoma Chef gives you real answers — grounded in the same local knowledge that built this map — not generic travel content.
